================================================================
System H — SACT (Syntax-Aware Cross-Lingual Transfer)
================================================================
                          (Part 1 Winner — Main Contribution)
================================================================

Description:
  Replace System G's blunt MSE alignment with three syntax-aware
  losses that operate at different levels of supervision:

    1. Content-word cosine alignment  (representation level,
       weighted toward nouns/verbs)
    2. Arc-distribution KL distillation (decision level — Hindi
       parser as teacher)
    3. Cross-lingual Tree Supervision (structural level — reuse
       Hindi gold heads on matched Bhojpuri tokens)

Backbone:           Frozen XLM-R + 2 language adapters
Trainable params:   ~200 K total
Training data:      Hindi-Bhojpuri aligned corpus (30,966 pairs)

----------------------------------------------------------------
Combined SACT objective
----------------------------------------------------------------
  L_H = L_bho
       + 0.5 * L_hi
       + 0.4 * L_cos
       + 2.0 * L_arc-kl
       + 0.2 * L_cts

----------------------------------------------------------------
Evaluation: Aligned Dev split (3,097 Bhojpuri sentences)
----------------------------------------------------------------
UAS:                55.85 %      <- Part 1 best
LAS:                50.08 %      <- Part 1 best

----------------------------------------------------------------
Improvement over System G
----------------------------------------------------------------
  ΔUAS at convergence:   +0.15 (55.70 → 55.85)
  ΔLAS at convergence:   +0.06 (50.02 → 50.08)
  ΔLAS at epoch 1:       +3.27 (44.31 → 47.58)  <- key win

  SACT converges ~5x faster than G. By epoch 2, H is already
  near its final score (49.81); G needs 10 epochs to catch up.
