================================================================
System F — Warm-Start Full Fine-Tuning (Baseline)
================================================================

Description:
  Initialise from the System A Hindi checkpoint, then fine-tune
  ALL parameters (~278M) on the 30,966-sentence Hindi-Bhojpuri
  aligned corpus using standard cross-entropy loss.

Backbone:           xlm-roberta-base (full fine-tune, no freezing)
Trainable params:   ~278 M
Training data:      Hindi-Bhojpuri aligned parallel corpus
                    (30,966 sentence pairs)
Warm start:         Yes (from System A's Hindi checkpoint)

----------------------------------------------------------------
Evaluation: Aligned Dev split (3,097 Bhojpuri sentences)
----------------------------------------------------------------
UAS:                27.57 %
LAS:                17.75 %

----------------------------------------------------------------
Why F is so weak
----------------------------------------------------------------
Three factors compound:

1. Full fine-tuning updates ~278M parameters on a small target
   corpus — overfitting risk is high.

2. No explicit cross-lingual alignment signal — the model must
   discover Hindi-Bhojpuri correspondences implicitly from the
   parsing loss alone.

3. Catastrophic forgetting — full fine-tuning erases the
   multilingual prior encoded in XLM-R during pretraining.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Role
----------------------------------------------------------------
F is the deliberate naive baseline for Part 1. Its weakness motivates
the frozen-backbone adapter design used in Systems G and H.

F → G yields a +32.3 LAS gain (17.75 → 50.02) just by freezing the
backbone and adding an explicit alignment loss. This is the most
dramatic improvement in the entire study.
