================================================================
System G — Parallel Adapters + MSE Alignment
================================================================
                          (First Novel Contribution)
================================================================

Description:
  Two key changes from System F:
    (1) Freeze XLM-R completely. Train only Pfeiffer adapters
        (r=64, ~100K params each) and biaffine heads.
    (2) Add an MSE positional alignment loss that pulls Bhojpuri
        hidden states toward Hindi hidden states at matched
        token positions.

Backbone:           Frozen XLM-R + 2 language adapters
Trainable params:   ~200 K total (1000x fewer than F)
Training data:      Hindi-Bhojpuri aligned corpus (30,966 pairs)

----------------------------------------------------------------
Training objective
----------------------------------------------------------------
  L_G = L_bho + 0.3 * L_hi + 0.5 * L_align(MSE)

  where L_align = (1/n) Σᵢ ||h_hi_i − h_bho_i||²

----------------------------------------------------------------
Evaluation: Aligned Dev split (3,097 Bhojpuri sentences)
----------------------------------------------------------------
UAS:                55.70 %
LAS:                50.02 %

----------------------------------------------------------------
Improvement over F
----------------------------------------------------------------
  ΔUAS:  +28.13 (27.57  → 55.70)
  ΔLAS:  +32.27 (17.75  → 50.02)

  With ~1000x fewer trainable parameters.

Role
----------------------------------------------------------------
First novel contribution — establishes the adapter-based architecture
template that System H (SACT) refines further. Demonstrates that
freezing the backbone + explicit alignment is a robust strategy for
low-resource cross-lingual transfer.
