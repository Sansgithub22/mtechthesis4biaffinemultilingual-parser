================================================================
System K — UD-Bridge via Silver Self-Training
================================================================
                          (Part 2 Winner — Benchmark Contribution)
================================================================

Description:
  Three-stage self-training pipeline that addresses the schema
  mismatch between auto-transferred labels (in the aligned corpus)
  and strict UD labels (in BHTB).

  Step 1: Run System A on 30,966 Bhojpuri sentences (FORM column
          only — labels stripped). Because System A operates in UD
          space, its predictions are UD-style by construction.
  Step 2: Concatenate the silver Bhojpuri (~30K sentences) with
          gold HDTB Hindi (~13K sentences). All training data now
          lives in the UD label space.
  Step 3: Train a fresh Trankit model on the combined corpus.

Backbone:           Trankit XLM-R (full fine-tune, no warm start)
Trainable params:   ~278 M
Training data:      HDTB gold (13,304) + Silver Bhojpuri UD (~30K)

----------------------------------------------------------------
Evaluation: BHTB gold test (357 sentences, strict UD v2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
UAS:                54.27 %      <- Part 2 best
LAS:                36.70 %      <- Part 2 best

----------------------------------------------------------------
Improvement over System A
----------------------------------------------------------------
  ΔUAS:  +1.49 (52.78 → 54.27)
  ΔLAS:  +1.34 (35.36 → 36.70)

  This is a new state-of-the-art on real Bhojpuri parsing.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Why K beats A
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Adds Bhojpuri-specific supervision through silver data that
   System A never sees during training.

2. Training labels and BHTB test labels are both in the UD schema
   — no schema mismatch, gains transfer directly to the benchmark.

3. HDTB Hindi gold acts as a regulariser, anchoring the model to
   correct UD structure and preventing silver-noise overfitting.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Statistical considerations on BHTB
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - BHTB has only 357 sentences (mean ~16 tokens), so a ~1.34 LAS
    gain corresponds to ~77 additional correctly-labelled tokens —
    well above plausible noise.
  - Multi-seed runs (seeds 42, 123, 7) show LAS std dev of ±0.4,
    smaller than the K-vs-A gap.
  - Both UAS (+1.49) and LAS (+1.34) move in the same direction,
    indicating systematic improvement.

----------------------------------------------------------------
The schema-consistency principle
----------------------------------------------------------------
Core claim: NOISY data in the RIGHT annotation space is more useful
than HIGH-QUALITY data in the WRONG annotation space.

This is why Systems G and H — which achieve 50%+ LAS on Aligned Dev
— score only ~6% LAS on BHTB. They are excellent parsers, but in
the wrong label dialect. Re-parsing in the UD space (System K) is
what unlocks BHTB performance.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Role
----------------------------------------------------------------
Part 2 winner. The benchmark contribution of the thesis. Validates
the "schema consistency beats data quantity" principle and provides
a methodologically clean pathway from zero-shot to low-resource
parsing on UD-style benchmarks.
